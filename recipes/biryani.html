Biryani

Description

Biryani is one of the most amazing royal delicacies introduced to Indians by the Persians. Since then it has been much popular and is considered to be a luxurious treat to enjoy on special occasions. Making a chicken biryani in authentic dum style does take some time and needs little expertise.

Ingredients

For Chicken Marination:

  • 500g chicken, bone-in
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1.5 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Juice of 1 lemon

For Rice:

  • 2 cups Basmati rice
  • 4-5 cups water
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt to taste

For Biryani Masala:

  • 2-3 tablespoons vegetable oil or ghee
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1-2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • Fresh coriander leaves, for garnish
  • Fresh mint leaves, for garnish

Steps:

Marinate Chicken:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, salt, garam masala, and lemon juice.
  2. Mix well and let it marinate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for best results.

Prepare Rice:

  1. Wash Basmati rice in cold water 2-3 times and soak for 30 minutes.
  2. In a large pot, bring water to a boil with cloves, cardamom, bay leaf, and salt.
  3. Add the soaked rice and cook until it's 70-80% done. Drain and set aside.

Prepare Biryani Masala:

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a large pot.
  2. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
  3. Add green chilies and ginger-garlic paste. Saut√© for a minute.
  4. Add red chili powder, turmeric, and garam masala. Mix well.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.

Assemble and Cook:

  1. In the pot with the biryani masala, layer the marinated chicken at the bottom.
  2. Add a layer of partially cooked rice on top of the chicken.
  3. Garnish with fresh coriander and mint leaves.
  4. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal with dough to trap the steam.
  5. Cook on low heat for 25-30 minutes.

Serve:

  1. Gently mix the biryani to combine the layers before serving.
  2. Serve hot with raita or salan.
